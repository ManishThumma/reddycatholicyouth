# Catholic Reddy Youth in America

This started at midnight with a coffee and one idea that I thought I would forget by morning.

I had just moved to a new city. New apartment, new job, new everything. You know how that goes. You are technically fine but the city does not feel like yours yet. Then out of nowhere I get a ping from a cousin I had not spoken to in probably two decades. She had seen through the family grapevine that I moved to the same city she was in. She invited me for lunch.

I showed up not knowing what to expect and she had cooked Telugu food. Real food. The kind that takes hours. We sat and talked like no time had passed at all. She introduced me to her kids, showed me around, and by the time I left I felt like I was home. Not the apartment. Home.

That was the moment. I kept thinking about how many of us are scattered across America right now. Cousins doing their Masters somewhere, cousins on H1Bs in cities they barely know anyone in, cousins who landed six months ago and are still figuring things out. All from the same family. All Catholic. All Telugu. All probably feeling at some point exactly what I was feeling before that lunch.

So I built this. A simple map. Your name, your city, your dot. So the next cousin who moves somewhere alone can open this and see that there is already family there.

We are Telugu Catholics from Telangana and Andhra Pradesh. Faith that goes back generations. The same rosary, the same feast days, the same village church weddings. Getting on a plane to America did not change any of that. If anything it made it mean more.

This is not a public directory. Every person on this map is a verified cousin. It stays within the family.

If you are family and you are not on the map yet, fill the form and you will be there within minutes.

Built on a weeknight with too much coffee and a lot of feeling.
